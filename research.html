Research

This page showcases my PhD research work in Vehicle Dynamics and Control Systems. Each section provides an overview, methodology, results, and links to related code or publications where available.

Suspension Modeling & Ride Comfort

My research focuses on modeling quarter-car and bicycle vehicle models to study ride comfort, handling, and stability. I simulate different suspension configurations and analyze their effects on vertical acceleration, pitch rate, and overall vehicle dynamics.

Tools used: MATLAB/Simulink, MSC ADAMS, SIMPACK.

Control Strategies for Active & Semi-Active Suspensions

I implement and compare multiple control strategies, including classical PID, LQR, MPC, Sliding Mode, Skyhook, Groundhook, and hybrid controllers, to optimize ride comfort and safety. Semi-active suspension research focuses on real-world feasible control forces.

Key highlights: - Side-by-side comparison of control strategies using simulation plots. - Modular Python and Simulink implementations for testing plug-and-play controllers.

Simulation Platforms & Methodology

I utilize advanced simulation platforms to model vehicle dynamics and validate control strategies. My workflow emphasizes reproducibility, modular code design, and clear visual outputs for analysis.

Platforms: Python, MATLAB/Simulink, MSC ADAMS, SIMPACK. Methodology: Step-by-step model building, system analysis, controller implementation, and performance evaluation.
